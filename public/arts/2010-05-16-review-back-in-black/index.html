Review: Back In Black

By Thais Baracat. May 16, 2010

In a fickle world, rock certainly remains. Bands from the 60’s, 70’s and 80’s are still being an influence for the recent ones, which are not based only on their musical style, but cultural aspects too. Memorable bestselling albums are from those decades, but one of them stands out: “Back In Black”, from AC/DC.

The album, which got the 26 place on Rolling Stone magazine’s list of The 100 Greatest Albums of the 80’s in 1989 and has already been ranked number 73 in the 500 greatest albums of all time from the same magazine, is colored in black due to a sign of mourning for Bon Scott, elder and legendary vocalist from AC/DC, place taken so far by Brian Johnson, whose voice became unmistakable since then.

In addition, some of the biggest hits from the band are contained in Back In Black, such as “Hells Bells”, “You Shook Me All Night Long” and the title track “Back In Black”. Other tracks which deserve a mention are “Rock and Roll Ain’t Noise Pollution” and “Shoot To Thrill”, which one has been used in Brazilian CQC’s intro.

Back In Black sounds like a regression to brand new but shaped rock period. Yet it doesn’t mean that you need to have been born a long time ago: if you enjoy the classic rock and don’t care about confessional and sentimental lyrics, you should give it a try.
